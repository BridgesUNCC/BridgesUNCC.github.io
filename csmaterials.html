CS Materials Workshop

Would you like to know how your CS course materials (lecture slides, assignments, exams, quizzes, etc.) measure up against national curriculum guidelines? And would you like to know how your course compares to equivalent courses taught by your colleagues? And are you interested in what their materials look like or you might want to adapt them to your course, such as a more appealing lecture slide set, practice quizzes or more engaging assignments?

At a SIGCSE workshop, there was a lot of discussion on how to meaningfully share class material and how to make them adoptable. How many times have you seen instructors try to share pedagogical material and reach only limited success?

Also, integrating parallel and distributed computing topics in Early CS require us to understand what Early CS courses look like across the national. (This is also true for any integration of content in early computing courses.)

We believe CS Materials could be an important tool to address these issues!

We are trying to organize a CS Materials workshop the Saturday before IPDPS. This workshop will show you how to input your material in the CS Materials system and how to use it to discover other materials that you could adopt. Be ready, at the workshop, to include the material from a class you teach in the system.

Since this workshop will be at IPDPS, we expect attendees to collectively bring in a mixture of parallel computing and systems courses, but also earlier CS courses possibly including some parallel computing topics integrating in these courses.

Stipends are available for workshop attendees.

Contact Erik Saule: esaule@uncc.edu for more information or to notify us that you are interested in such a workshop.

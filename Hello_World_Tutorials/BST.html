How does the BSTElement<K,E> work?

BSTElement<K,E> is another type of container that has two pointers that point to two child BSTElement<K,E>. It uses the same conventions as the TreeElement<E>.

At this point, you may be wondering how a BSTElement<K,E> differs from a regular TreeElement<E>. The fundamental difference between the two classes is that the BSTElement<K,E> holds an additional piece of data, K, called the "key". The key is used to enforce a logical ordering on the tree structure independent of the element that is being held.

A typical example would be a binary search tree where all of the keys of the left child are smaller than the keys of the right child.



Notice that the key for BST1 is smaller than the key for the root so it's to the left of the root. Also notice that the key for BST3 is smaller than the key for BST2, but larger than the key for the root. So BST3 sits on the left child of BST2, but in the right child of the root.
